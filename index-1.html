FAQ

Common questions

Is MindMate a replacement for therapy? ▾
No. MindMate is a supportive companion and wellness tool, not a licensed therapist. We always encourage students to seek professional support when needed. MindMate is designed to fill the gap between appointments, provide 24/7 support, and help students recognize when professional care is the right next step.
Is my data private and secure? ▾
Absolutely. All conversations are end-to-end encrypted. We are fully HIPAA compliant and never sell or share your data with third parties. You can delete your entire account and all associated data at any time.
What happens during a mental health crisis? ▾
MindMate uses natural language understanding to detect signs of serious distress. When detected, it immediately offers emergency resources, crisis hotlines, and can help connect you to campus counseling. In severe situations, it will always direct you to call emergency services (911).
Is there a free plan? ▾
Yes! Our free plan includes daily mood check-ins, up to 20 AI conversations per day, 3 guided exercises, and basic journaling. It's genuinely useful on its own — not a watered-down trial. Upgrade to Pro for unlimited chat, advanced analytics, and 50+ exercises.
What languages does MindMate support? ▾
MindMate is currently available in 12 languages: English, Spanish, French, German, Mandarin, Hindi, Arabic, Portuguese, Japanese, Korean, Italian, and Russian. We're expanding this list based on user demand.

Our Story

MindMate was born from the experience of students who couldn't afford therapy, couldn't get appointments fast enough, and felt alone at 2am with their anxiety.

Our Mission

Mental health support for every student, everywhere

1 in 3 college students experiences significant mental health challenges. Yet fewer than 25% ever seek help — because of cost, stigma, waitlists, or not knowing where to start.

MindMate believes that technology, used thoughtfully, can bridge that gap. Not to replace human connection or professional care — but to ensure no student faces their hardest moments alone.
